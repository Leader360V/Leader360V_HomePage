Leader360V Dataset

Overview

Leader360V is the first large-scale (10K+), labeled real-world 360 video datasets for instance segmentation and tracking. Our datasets enjoy high scene diversity, ranging from indoor and urban settings to natural and dynamic outdoor scenes.

All videos in this dataset have undergone standardized preprocessing, including video clipping, facial anonymization for privacy protection, and balanced scene distribution across categories.

Regarding dataset composition, we integrated existing 360 video datasets (either unlabeled or annotated for single tasks) and supplemented them with newly collected self-recorded videos. All content was then re-annotated to support joint segmentation and tracking tasks.

Experiments demonstrate that models trained on Leader360V achieve superior performance in 360 video segmentation and tracking, with significant improvements in task accuracy.

By providing richly annotated data for 360 video scene understanding and demonstrating versatility across downstream tasks, Leader360V advances research frontiers in related domains while enabling the development of more generalized 360 video comprehension models. This progress offers robust support for applications in autonomous driving, robotics, virtual reality, and beyond. Looking ahead, we plan to further expand the dataset's scope to accommodate an even broader spectrum of research tasks.

Following paper acceptance, we will publicly release all code and datasets for free use by researchers in academia and industry.
